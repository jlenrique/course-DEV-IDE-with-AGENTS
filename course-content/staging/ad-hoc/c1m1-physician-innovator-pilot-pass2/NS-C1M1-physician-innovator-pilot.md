[SEGMENT: seg-01]

**[Gary Slide: S01 — Title card]**

**Stage directions:** Welcoming, confident; pace slow on the title.

**Narration:** Welcome to Course One, Module One. This isn’t a detour from your clinical identity — it’s an extension of it. Over the next few minutes we’ll make a single argument plain: *the physician as innovator* is not a separate job title you apply for. It’s a way of seeing the system you already work inside — and a set of skills you can develop without leaving the mission that brought you to medicine.

**Transition:** That claim only lands if we name the tension you’re living in today.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-02]

**[Gary Slide: S02 — Modern clinician dilemma]**

**Stage directions:** Credible, slightly sober; avoid cynicism.

**Narration:** Medicine is accelerating — evidence, technology, and operational complexity don’t wait for anyone’s comfort level. Many of us were trained deeply in *how to deliver care along established pathways* — and far less often in how to *redesign* those pathways when they fail patients or teams. The gap isn’t usually a shortage of good intentions. It’s the combination of **structured process** for change and **psychological safety** to name what’s broken. Until those exist, even excellent clinicians stay stuck in reactive mode.

**Transition:** So we start by naming the mindset that flips the frame.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-03]

**[Gary Slide: S03 — Innovation mindset]**

**Stage directions:** Definitional clarity; emphasize *designer*, not *hero*.

**Narration:** In this program, an **innovation mindset** means this: you hold the internal conviction that you can be a **designer of the healthcare system** — not only an employee within it. You’re still bound by evidence, ethics, and scope of practice. You’re *expanding* where your agency applies: to workflows, handoffs, and system patterns that either help or harm. The National Academy of Medicine and related work on clinician well-being remind us: burnout isn’t only personal resilience — it’s often **system design**. That’s the arena we’re entering.

**Transition:** You’re not taking this journey alone — here’s the arc.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-04]

**[Gary Slide: S04 — Your journey]**

**Stage directions:** Forward motion; orient without hype.

**Narration:** Think of this certificate as a **hero’s journey** with three movements. Course One — where you are now — is **Sparking**: learning to see friction and opportunity in the clinical world you already inhabit. Course Two is **Building**: design, planning, and pitch. Course Three is **Driving impact**: turning projects into durable change. The point isn’t a binder of slides — it’s a **playbook** you can use and an executive one-pager you can defend. *You* sit at the center of that arc.

**Transition:** Before frameworks, remember: you’re walking in a long lineage.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-05]

**[Gary Slide: S05 — Physician innovators]**

**Stage directions:** Pride, precision; names are anchors, not trivia.

**Narration:** When we say physicians innovate, we’re not speaking abstractly. **Thomas Fogarty** helped transform vascular surgery with device thinking. **Peter Pronovost** turned a checklist into a global patient-safety movement — that’s **process** innovation at scale. **Patricia Bath** advanced how we treat cataracts — technology tied to access. And at the **organizational** level, health systems have shifted from purely reactive sick care toward models that try to engineer health. Different levers — same through-line: physicians who refused to treat broken systems as immutable.

**Transition:** The bridge to you is shorter than it feels.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-06]

**[Gary Slide: S06 — Physicianship + innovation]**

**Stage directions:** Reassuring; lower the “I’m not a founder” barrier.

**Narration:** Here’s the reframe many clinicians need: **physicianship** and **innovation leadership** draw on the same muscle groups — *empathy* for who suffers, *curiosity* when data surprises you, *analytical discipline* when noise is loud, *comfort with ambiguity* when the right next step isn’t obvious, and *resilience* when the first fix fails. You are not being asked to become someone else. You’re being asked to aim traits you already exercise at the bedside toward **systems and operations** — where harm and waste also hide.

**Transition:** If that sounds abstract, map it to something you do twenty times a day.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-07]

**[Gary Slide: S07 — Clinical method / design thinking]**

**Stage directions:** Deliberate; this is the conceptual hinge — let silence do work between clauses.

**Narration:** **Design thinking** can sound like business jargon — until you map it to your clinical method. When you take a history, you’re **building empathy with reality** — what’s actually happening to this person. When you synthesize exam and data into a working diagnosis, you’re **defining the problem** with rigor. When you build a differential and choose a plan, you’re **ideating** and **testing** a hypothesis — then iterating when the story changes. You already run an innovation loop; the “patient” is often a human being. In this program, we’ll also aim that loop at **broken workflows** — where harm is statistical, not singular.

**Transition:** Where you deploy that loop matters for leverage.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-08]

**[Gary Slide: S08 — Intrapreneur vs entrepreneur]**

**Stage directions:** Pragmatic; respect institutional reality.

**Narration:** **Entrepreneurship** and **intrapreneurship** are cousins, not duplicates. An entrepreneur often builds something new from the outside. An **intrapreneur** drives innovation **inside** an existing organization — navigating politics, capital, brand, and scale that already exist. For many of you, the fastest path to impact is intrapreneurial: improve the system that already trusts you with patients. That requires stakeholder alignment — a skill we’ll return to, especially around your capstone.

**Transition:** The first discipline gate is vocabulary — idea versus opportunity.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-09]

**[Gary Slide: S09 — Idea vs opportunity]**

**Stage directions:** Sharp, slightly challenging — “we’re not collecting slogans.”

**Narration:** An **idea** is a sentence in a meeting — “we should use AI for notes,” “we should fix boarding,” “we should redesign sign-out.” Useful — and *insufficient*. An **opportunity** is an idea that has been tested against **desirability, feasibility, and viability** in *your* environment, with a credible path to execution. This program is designed to move you from brainstorming to **engineering opportunities** — because healthcare already has enough unfunded mandates dressed up as innovation.

**Transition:** Close with what you’ll actually carry forward from Module One.

---

[SEGMENT: seg-10]

**[Gary Slide: S10 — Next steps]**

**Stage directions:** Open door; concrete without overwhelming.

**Narration:** Here’s the honest summary. Clinical training makes you an expert in care delivery; it does not automatically make you an expert in **system innovation** — and that gap is fixable. Your mission in this module is to strengthen **process**, **psychological safety**, and **first-principles thinking** — and to start capturing friction you see every week. Upcoming work will walk you toward an **Opportunity Radar** and a personal **SWOT** — simple tools that turn passive frustration into something you can prioritize and test. The rest of the journey turns those seeds into plans, pitches, and impact. For now: notice what breaks — and assume you’re allowed to care.

**Transition:** End of pilot narration block — proceed to voice generation and assembly.
