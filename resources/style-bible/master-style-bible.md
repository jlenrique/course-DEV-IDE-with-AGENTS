# Master Style Bible

This file is the authoritative brand, voice, and accessibility reference for APP production runs.

## Audience

- Adult learners in asynchronous online health sciences and medical education contexts
- Respect clinician expertise while keeping explanations teachable for learners earlier in their training arc

## Voice And Tone

- Clear, direct, evidence-aware
- Respectful of physician and clinical expertise
- Practical rather than promotional
- Inclusive and jargon-aware: define specialized language when it first matters
- Confident without hype

## Visual Identity

### Core Colors

- JCPH Navy: `#1e3a5f`
- Medical Teal: `#4a90a4`
- White: `#ffffff`
- Warm gray for secondary surfaces and dividers: `#d9dee5`
- Dark body text: `#1f2933`

### Typography

- Headlines: Montserrat
- Body: Open Sans
- Data / dense labels: Source Sans Pro

### Imagery Standards

- Favor professional, contemporary healthcare and academic imagery
- Prefer purposeful, instructional visuals over decorative stock imagery
- Avoid sensationalized clinical scenes, gratuitous suffering, or consumer-tech hype aesthetics
- Literal-visual slides should preserve the approved source meaning without stylistic drift

## Slide And Media Guidance

- Prioritize one primary teaching move per slide
- Keep dense text slides legible; motion must never reduce readability on literal-text or literal-visual slides
- When a motion clip is approved for playback, keep the canonical slide still as the approved slide reference and track the clip separately as the playback asset
- Storyboard review for motion segments should expose both the approved still and the approved clip

## Writing Standards

- Lead with the instructional point, then support it with evidence, framing, or examples
- Prefer concrete verbs and specific claims over abstract filler
- Preserve source-faithful meaning when working in literal-text or literal-visual modes
- Narration should complement visuals, not merely restate visible text
- For motion-first segments, orient briefly to the slide if needed, then narrate the visible action/change in the approved clip

## Accessibility

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast
- Minimum contrast goal for normal text: `4.5:1`
- Use logical heading structure and stable reading order
- Provide alt text or equivalent descriptive treatment for meaningful visuals
- Caption spoken audio
- Avoid motion that introduces unnecessary cognitive load or distracts from essential text

## Quality Checklist

- Brand colors and typography remain within the approved palette/system
- Voice is clear, respectful, and evidence-aware
- Visuals support the instructional objective rather than decorate it
- Literal fidelity modes preserve source meaning
- Accessibility expectations are met or explicitly escalated for review
