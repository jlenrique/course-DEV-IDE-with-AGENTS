## Audio briefing (from Trial 1 feedback)

Vary narration segment LENGTH deliberately. Trial 1 audio was "very good" but too uniform in duration. For Trial 2:
- **Heavy conceptual slides** (burnout data, knowledge explosion, leadership gap) → longer narration segments (90–120 seconds)
- **Transitional slides** (title, forward pull) → shorter segments (30–45 seconds)
- **Mid-weight slides** (consumer shift, digital front door case) → moderate segments (60–90 seconds)

This creates natural pacing variety and signals to learners which content is foundational vs. connective.

## Instructional strategy

Single narrative arc: **the system is broken in specific, measurable ways** → **the breakage creates both suffering and opportunity** → **physicians are uniquely positioned to lead the fix, but need structured innovation training to do it**. The emotional spine moves from *sober data* to *human cost* to *technological promise tempered by training gap* to *empowering synthesis*. Data-heavy slides anchor credibility; the closing reframes data as a call to action.

Cognitive load management: one macro trend per slide; no multi-axis data walls; key statistics isolated as hero numbers. The SME notes are dense — this lesson plan spreads 5 SME slides into 10 narrated slides for digestibility.

### Block 2: The healthcare economy — spending and structural shifts

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (economic forces)
- **Bloom's Level:** Analyze
- **Content Type:** data visualization slide
- **Estimated Duration:** 90–120 sec narration (data-heavy)
- **Key Terminology:** NHE $5.2 trillion, independent practice, hospital employment, enterprise navigation
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Establish scale; sobering but not demoralizing

**Content Summary:** Two linked data points: (1) healthcare spending trajectory ($5.2T, ~1/5 GDP), (2) structural shift from independent practice to hospital employment (2012–2022). Conclusion: you now work inside massive organizations, so innovation requires enterprise navigation skills.

**Cognitive Load Notes:** Two statistics maximum on slide; narration provides context and implications.

---

### Block 3: The human cost — administrative waste and burnout

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (burnout as system design problem)
- **Bloom's Level:** Analyze
- **Content Type:** infographic with contrasting hero numbers
- **Estimated Duration:** 90–120 sec narration (emotional anchor + data)
- **Key Terminology:** 25% admin waste, 50%+ burnout, bureaucratic friction, system design problem, EHR workarounds
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Validate the learner's daily frustration; reframe burnout as design failure (not personal weakness)

**Content Summary:** Two stark numbers: 25% of spending → administrative waste; 50%+ physicians → burnout symptoms. The critical reframe: burnout's primary driver is bureaucratic tasks, not patient volume. "The workarounds you invent daily are the friction points ripe for innovation."

**Cognitive Load Notes:** This is the **emotional anchor** slide — let the numbers land before pivoting to the reframe. Narration should pause after the statistics.

---

### Block 4: The knowledge explosion

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (technological acceleration)
- **Bloom's Level:** Analyze
- **Content Type:** timeline or exponential growth visualization
- **Estimated Duration:** 90–120 sec narration (data-heavy + implications)
- **Key Terminology:** 50 years → 73 days, medical knowledge doubling, static training obsolescence
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Urgency without panic; "the ground is shifting under traditional training"

**Content Summary:** Medical knowledge doubling time: 50 years (1950) → 73 days (projected). Static training cannot keep pace. This is not a future problem — it is happening now.

**Cognitive Load Notes:** One hero number (73 days) anchors the slide; narration provides the 1950 comparison and implications.

---

### Block 5: Technology — promise and the training gap

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (tech acceleration + AI gap)
- **Bloom's Level:** Analyze / Evaluate
- **Content Type:** split: upside evidence + gap callout
- **Estimated Duration:** 75–90 sec narration (moderate)
- **Key Terminology:** 66% AI adoption, SMART remote monitoring, VR procedural training, training gap
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Balance: "technology is transforming care AND we're not ready to lead it"

**Content Summary:** Upside examples: SMART program reducing ER visits (COPD/HF), VR training for procedures. Gap: 66% using AI, but formal oversight/evaluation training severely limited. Technology will reshape care; it requires clinical innovators to steer it safely.

**Cognitive Load Notes:** Keep upside and gap on the same slide to create productive tension. Do not resolve the tension — that's the lesson's job.

### Block 6: The consumer shift

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (healthcare consumerism)
- **Bloom's Level:** Analyze
- **Content Type:** concept introduction + patient journey
- **Estimated Duration:** 60–75 sec narration (moderate)
- **Key Terminology:** healthcare consumerism, digital access, transparency, patient-as-consumer, Digital Front Door
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Expand the physician's frame beyond clinical quality to system experience

**Content Summary:** Patients now expect transparency, digital access, and convenience comparable to other industries. Introduce the "Digital Front Door" concept: not just a patient portal — a frictionless digital ecosystem.

**Cognitive Load Notes:** Concept-only slide; the case study follows on the next slide.

### Block 7: Digital Front Door in action — Jefferson Health

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (consumerism applied)
- **Bloom's Level:** Apply / Analyze
- **Content Type:** brief case study (single organization)
- **Estimated Duration:** 60–75 sec narration (moderate)
- **Key Terminology:** Jefferson Health, AI-driven triage, comprehensive digital ecosystem, intentional system design
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Make the abstract concrete; "this is what innovation-led design looks like"

**Content Summary:** Jefferson Health's comprehensive digital ecosystem — AI-driven triage, seamless patient navigation, beyond basic telehealth. Takeaway: "Intentional system design separates a frustrating experience from a world-class one."

**Cognitive Load Notes:** One example, told well. Do not list multiple health systems.

### Block 8: The leadership gap

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (culminating gap analysis)
- **Bloom's Level:** Analyze / Evaluate
- **Content Type:** gap visualization (bar chart or contrast)
- **Estimated Duration:** 90–120 sec narration (data-heavy + synthesis)
- **Key Terminology:** 67% leadership interest, 18% formal training, COVID-19 management skills, Academic Health Centers, interdisciplinary integration
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** "This gap is why you're here — and why this program exists"

**Content Summary:** The culminating data point: 67% of physicians want leadership roles; only 18% receive any business training. COVID showed management skills are critical, not "soft." Academic Health Centers have expertise but lack the cultural infrastructure. This slide answers "why this program" at the structural level.

**Cognitive Load Notes:** The 67% vs 18% contrast is the **headline number** for the entire lesson. Let it dominate the slide.

### Block 9: The case for change — synthesis

- **Objective Served:** CLO2 (synthesis)
- **Bloom's Level:** Evaluate
- **Content Type:** summary / recap with synthesis statement
- **Estimated Duration:** 75–90 sec narration (moderate-heavy)
- **Key Terminology:** system strain, human toll, pace of change, guided innovation, frontline clinicians
- **Behavioral / Affective Goal:** Transform data burden into empowerment; "you are uniquely positioned"

**Content Summary:** Four-point synthesis: (1) System Strain — costs, waste, consolidation. (2) Human Toll — bureaucratic friction drives burnout. (3) Pace of Change — knowledge and AI outpace training. (4) The Opportunity — incremental adaptation is insufficient; system-aware innovation must come from frontline clinicians. Closing narration: "You already navigate uncertainty and solve complex problems in real-time. It is time to translate that into tools for shaping the future of care." Pass 2 narration should also include a brief Part 3 forward pull ("next, we move from *why* to *how* — structured tools for turning insight into vetted opportunity").

**Cognitive Load Notes:** Recap structure — no new data. Four short bullets + one synthesis statement. Forward pull language added to narration (Pass 2) since Slide 10 now serves as a knowledge check teaser instead.
