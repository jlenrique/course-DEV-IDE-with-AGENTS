## Slide Specifications

### Slide 1

**Content:**
- **Title:** The Macro Trends & The Case for Change
- **Subtitle:** Why physician-led innovation is no longer optional
- **Course line:** C1 · Module 1 · Part 2

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: title-plus-body
- Hero Element: title + subtitle
- Visual Density: minimal
- Image Guidance: abstract healthcare environment, cool blues/navy, professional — forward-leaning energy (not crisis imagery)

**Learning Purpose:** Frame the analytical arc; signal shift from "what" (Part 1) to "why."

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Analytical confidence — "we're about to look at the data."

---

### Slide 2

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The healthcare economy
- **Hero numbers:**
  - **$5.2 trillion** — U.S. healthcare spending (2024), nearly 1/5 of GDP
  - **Structural shift:** Independent practice declining → hospital employment surging (2012–2022)
- **Takeaway line:** To effect change, you must navigate and influence large enterprises.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: two data callouts side by side (spending trend + practice model shift)
- Hero Element: $5.2T number
- Visual Density: balanced (two data points, no clutter)
- Image Guidance: clean data visualization aesthetic — navy background, teal accent, white typography. Upward trending line + contrasting bars.

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 — establish the economic and structural forces reshaping practice.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Sobering but clear — "this is the environment you work in."

---

### Slide 3

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The human cost
- **Hero numbers:**
  - **25%** of U.S. healthcare spending → administrative waste (not patient care)
  - **50%+** of physicians → burnout symptoms
- **Reframe (on slide):** The #1 driver of burnout is bureaucratic tasks — not patient volume. **Burnout is a system design problem.**
- **Pull line:** Your daily workarounds are friction points ripe for innovation.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: two stark hero numbers + reframe statement below
- Hero Element: the two percentages, then the reframe
- Visual Density: minimal-balanced (numbers need visual weight)
- Image Guidance: high contrast — dark background, large white/teal numbers, red or warm accent for burnout stat if appropriate

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 — reframe burnout from personal failing to design opportunity. This is the **emotional anchor** of the lesson.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Validating — "your frustration is a data point, not a weakness."

---

### Slide 4

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The knowledge explosion
- **Hero contrast:**
  - 1950: medical knowledge doubled every **50 years**
  - Today: projected doubling every **73 days**
- **Implication:** Static training cannot keep pace. Continuous learning and innovation frameworks are essential.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: timeline or exponential curve — 1950 anchor → present projection
- Hero Element: 50 years → 73 days contrast
- Visual Density: balanced
- Image Guidance: exponential curve visual, clean and modern. Navy/teal palette. No clip art.

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 — technological acceleration as a structural force.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Urgency without panic — "the ground is shifting."

---

### Slide 5

**Content:**
- **Headline:** Technology: promise and the training gap
- **Two columns or split:**
  - **Promise:** SMART remote monitoring → reduced ER visits (COPD/HF); VR → risk-free procedural training
  - **Gap:** 66% of physicians using AI — formal training on oversight/evaluation severely limited
- **Takeaway:** Technology will reshape care; it requires clinical innovators to ensure it does so safely.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: split (left: upside, right: gap) or balance visual
- Hero Element: the 66% + "severely limited" contrast
- Visual Density: balanced
- Image Guidance: innovation + caution dual tone — teal for promise, navy for gap

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 — productive tension between technological potential and readiness.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Balanced alarm — "the tools are here; the training isn't."

---

### Slide 6

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The consumer shift
- **Bullets (short):**
  - Patients expect transparency, digital access, and convenience — like every other industry
  - Organizations must rethink access infrastructure
  - The "Digital Front Door": not a patient portal — a frictionless digital ecosystem
- **Takeaway:** Intentional system design separates frustrating from world-class.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: title-plus-body with optional patient journey icon strip (smartphone → triage → provider)
- Hero Element: "Digital Front Door" concept
- Visual Density: minimal-balanced
- Image Guidance: patient journey visual, clean and modern

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 — healthcare consumerism as structural force.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Expanding the frame — "innovation isn't just clinical; it's experiential."

---

### Slide 7

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The Digital Front Door in action
- **Case:** Jefferson Health
  - Beyond basic telehealth → comprehensive digital ecosystem
  - AI-driven triage for seamless patient navigation
  - Frictionless journey from first contact to care
- **Closing line:** This is what innovation-led system design looks like.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: brief case study format — organization name + 2-3 key features
- Hero Element: Jefferson Health name + "comprehensive digital ecosystem"
- Visual Density: minimal
- Image Guidance: clean, institutional — could show a simplified patient flow diagram

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 applied — concrete example of consumer-shift innovation.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Aspirational — "this already exists; your organization could do this."

---

### Slide 8

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The leadership gap
- **Hero contrast:**
  - **67%** of physicians want leadership roles
  - **18%** receive any formal business or executive training
- **Supporting points:**
  - COVID proved management skills are critical, not "soft"
  - Academic Health Centers: deep clinical expertise, but lack cultural infrastructure for innovation
- **Closing line:** We need individuals who bridge clinical practice with business and technology strategy.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: two hero numbers (bar chart or side-by-side callout) + supporting bullets below
- Hero Element: **67% vs 18%** (this is the headline number for the entire lesson)
- Visual Density: balanced
- Image Guidance: gap visualization — large contrasting bars or numbers, navy/teal, high contrast

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 — the culminating "why you are here" data point.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Motivating — "this gap is the reason this program exists."

---

### Slide 9

**Content:**
- **Headline:** The case for change
- **Four-point synthesis:**
  1. **System Strain** — Rising costs, admin waste, and consolidation are changing practice models
  2. **Human Toll** — Bureaucratic friction is the primary driver of burnout
  3. **Pace of Change** — Knowledge and AI growth outpace traditional training
  4. **The Opportunity** — Incremental adaptation is insufficient; guided, system-aware innovation must come from frontline clinicians
- **Closing statement:** You already navigate uncertainty and solve complex problems in real-time. Translate that into tools for shaping the future of care.

**Visual Guidance:**
- Layout: four numbered points + bold closing
- Hero Element: the four-point framework
- Visual Density: balanced (recap density is expected here)
- Image Guidance: clean summary layout — could use four small icons per point

**Learning Purpose:** CLO2 synthesis — consolidate the argument into a portable framework.

**Behavioral / Affective Intent:** Empowering — data burden transformed into mandate.
