# Exemplar: Check Your Understanding

## Level: L4.1 (Complex)

Single slide. Interactive assessment-style slide with a question prompt and categorization exercise.

## What This Is

A single Gamma slide that presents a comprehension check activity. It asks students to categorize skills (Active Listening, Data Analysis, Experimentation) into whether they belong to clinical practice, innovation, or both. This is a pedagogical assessment slide, not a content delivery slide.

## Content

**Title**: Check Your Understanding

**Prompt**: Which skills belong to clinical practice, innovation, or both?

**Items to categorize**: Active Listening, Data Analysis, Experimentation

## Why It's Good

- Shifts from passive content to active engagement
- Simple categorization exercise that reinforces the parallel between clinical practice and innovation
- Three items keep cognitive load manageable
- The question is open-ended enough to provoke discussion but structured enough for self-check
- Follows Bloom's taxonomy: moving from understanding (L1-L3) to applying/analyzing (L4)

## Context Relationship

- Course: Applied Professional Communication / Innovation in Medicine
- Audience: Medical professionals checking their comprehension
- Learning objective: Students can correctly identify where clinical and innovation skills overlap
- Pedagogical approach: Formative assessment — testing understanding mid-lesson

## What the Agent Should Learn

1. Gamma can produce assessment/interactive-style slides
2. The challenge is getting Gamma to format this as an activity, not just content
3. `additionalInstructions` must explicitly request "interactive assessment" or "comprehension check" formatting
4. Assessment slides need different visual treatment — question prominence, clear item separation
5. This is harder than content slides because the layout serves a pedagogical function, not just information delivery

## Reproduction Notes

- `numCards: 1`, `textMode: preserve`
- `additionalInstructions`: emphasize interactive/assessment layout, question-and-options format
- The three items should be visually distinct and positioned for categorization thinking
- Success = recognizable as an assessment slide, not a content slide; question is prominent; items are clearly presented
