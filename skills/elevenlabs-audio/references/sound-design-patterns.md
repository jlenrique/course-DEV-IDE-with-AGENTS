# Sound Design Patterns

## Production Principle

Narration is the primary signal. Sound effects and music are supportive only.

## Sound Effects

Appropriate uses:
- Transition emphasis
- Module bumper sting
- Light interface or notification cue

Avoid:
- Constant decorative whooshes
- Dense effects under explanation
- Anything that competes with narration intelligibility

## Music

Appropriate uses:
- Brief intro/outro
- Low instrumental underscore when the content genuinely benefits from it

Avoid:
- Full music beds under dense explanatory narration unless explicitly requested
- Lyrics in instructional content
- Emotional manipulation that fights the lesson tone

## Dialogue

- Use multi-speaker dialogue for case studies, debates, and role-play scenarios.
- Keep speaker identity explicit via segment-level or input-level `voice_id` values.
- If a use case can be served by straightforward narration, narration is the safer default.
