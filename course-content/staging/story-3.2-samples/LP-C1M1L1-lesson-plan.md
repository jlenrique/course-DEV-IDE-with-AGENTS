# Lesson Plan: Defining Innovative Mindset

## Header

- **Module:** M1 — Foundations of Innovation Leadership
- **Lesson:** L1 — Defining Innovative Mindset
- **Content Strand:** IS-1
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - LO1: Define key characteristics of an innovative mindset (flexibility, resiliency, adaptability) [Bloom's: Remember]
  - LO2: Explain how comfort with ambiguity and failure contribute to innovation capacity [Bloom's: Understand]
  - LO3: Apply first-principles thinking to deconstruct a healthcare problem [Bloom's: Apply]
- **Estimated Duration:** 25 minutes
- **Prerequisites:** None (opening lesson)
- **Pairing Reference:** LP-C1M1L1

## Content Blocks

### Block 1: Hook — "The Physician Who Failed Forward"

- **Objective Served:** LO2 (comfort with failure)
- **Bloom's Level:** Understand
- **Content Type:** First-person explainer
- **Writer Delegation:** Sophia — narrative engagement, authentic clinical voice
- **Estimated Duration:** 3 minutes
- **Key Terminology:** growth mindset, psychological safety, fail forward

**Content Summary:**
A brief first-person account from an experienced physician who describes a clinical innovation that failed initially but led to a breakthrough approach. Sets the emotional tone: failure in innovation is data, not defeat.

**Cognitive Load Notes:**
Single narrative thread, no branching. Listener focuses on one story arc. Dual-coding: narration paired with minimal visual (one key slide).

### Block 2: Concept Introduction — Core Characteristics

- **Objective Served:** LO1 (define characteristics)
- **Bloom's Level:** Remember
- **Content Type:** Structured explanation
- **Writer Delegation:** Paige — precise technical definitions
- **Estimated Duration:** 7 minutes
- **Key Terminology:** flexibility, resiliency, adaptability, innovative mindset, growth mindset, fixed mindset

**Content Summary:**
Define the four characteristics of an innovative mindset: flexibility (willingness to change approach), resiliency (recovery from setbacks), adaptability (adjusting to new conditions), and comfort with ambiguity (operating without complete information). Contrast with fixed mindset. Use healthcare examples for each.

**Cognitive Load Notes:**
Four concepts chunked as a natural group. Use visual table or comparison layout on slides. Each concept: definition → healthcare example → contrast.

### Block 3: Elaboration — Working with Ambiguity

- **Objective Served:** LO2 (explain ambiguity's role)
- **Bloom's Level:** Understand
- **Content Type:** Narrated case scenario
- **Writer Delegation:** Sophia — clinical scenario with ambiguity as the central theme
- **Estimated Duration:** 5 minutes
- **Key Terminology:** ambiguity tolerance, uncertainty management, clinical decision-making under uncertainty

**Content Summary:**
A clinical scenario where a physician must make innovation decisions with incomplete market data — paralleling clinical decision-making under uncertainty. The learner sees how the same cognitive skills used in clinical judgment apply to innovation leadership.

**Cognitive Load Notes:**
Single scenario with one decision point. Scaffolded: familiar (clinical uncertainty) → novel (innovation uncertainty). Dual-coding: narration + scenario diagram on slide.

### Block 4: Application — First Principles Thinking Exercise

- **Objective Served:** LO3 (apply first-principles thinking)
- **Bloom's Level:** Apply
- **Content Type:** Guided exercise with narration
- **Writer Delegation:** Paige — structured procedure steps
- **Estimated Duration:** 7 minutes
- **Key Terminology:** first principles, decomposition, root cause, assumptions, healthcare innovation

**Content Summary:**
Walk the learner through a first-principles analysis of a healthcare problem (e.g., "Why do patients miss follow-up appointments?"). Step-by-step: identify assumptions → strip to fundamentals → rebuild from base truths → generate novel approach. Learner is guided to apply the framework mentally as the narration progresses.

**Cognitive Load Notes:**
Scaffolded 4-step process. Each step builds on the previous. Visual: step-by-step diagram accumulating on slides. No more than one new concept per step.

### Block 5: Synthesis and Assessment

- **Objective Served:** LO1, LO2, LO3
- **Bloom's Level:** Remember/Understand (formative check)
- **Content Type:** Assessment
- **Writer Delegation:** N/A (assessment design by Irene)
- **Estimated Duration:** 3 minutes
- **Key Terminology:** All from blocks 1-4

**Content Summary:**
Three-question formative assessment checking retention of key characteristics (LO1), understanding of ambiguity's role (LO2), and ability to identify first-principles steps (LO3).
