## Assessment Items

### Item 1: Characteristic Recognition (LO1, Remember)

**Question Stem:**
Which of the following is NOT one of the four core characteristics of an innovative mindset as defined in this lesson?

**Correct Answer:**
D) Perfectionism

**Bloom's Justification:**
Tests recall of the four defined characteristics. The learner must recognize which belongs to the set and which does not.

**Distractors:**

| Option | Why It's Plausibly Wrong |
|--------|------------------------|
| A) Flexibility | Correct characteristic — distractor for learners who confuse the list |
| B) Resiliency | Correct characteristic |
| C) Comfort with ambiguity | Correct characteristic |
| D) Perfectionism | Seems related to excellence but is actually antithetical to innovative mindset — learners with fixed mindset orientation may select this |

**Feedback:**
- Correct: "Right — perfectionism is a fixed-mindset trait. The four characteristics are flexibility, resiliency, adaptability, and comfort with ambiguity."
- Incorrect: "Review the four characteristics: flexibility, resiliency, adaptability, and comfort with ambiguity. Perfectionism is actually a barrier to innovation."

### Item 2: Ambiguity's Role (LO2, Understand)

**Question Stem:**
A physician developing a patient-engagement app receives mixed pilot data after three weeks. According to this lesson, the most productive response is to:

**Correct Answer:**
B) Treat the data as a preliminary differential and design targeted follow-up to narrow hypotheses

**Bloom's Justification:**
Tests understanding of how clinical reasoning frameworks (differential diagnosis) apply to innovation uncertainty. Requires interpretation, not just recall.

**Distractors:**

| Option | Why It's Plausibly Wrong |
|--------|------------------------|
| A) Conclude the app was built for the wrong audience and start over | Premature conclusion from insufficient data — common fixed-mindset response |
| B) Treat the data as a preliminary differential and design targeted follow-up | Correct — applies ambiguity tolerance with structured reasoning |
| C) Wait for six months of data before making any decisions | Over-cautious — avoids ambiguity rather than working with it |
| D) Ignore the data and trust the original market research | Dismisses evidence — opposite of adaptability |

### Item 3: First-Principles Ordering (LO3, Apply)

**Question Stem:**
Arrange the following steps of first-principles thinking in the correct order:

1. Rebuild from base truths
2. Identify your assumptions
3. Generate a novel approach
4. Strip to fundamentals

**Correct Answer:**
2 → 4 → 1 → 3 (Identify assumptions → Strip to fundamentals → Rebuild from base truths → Generate novel approach)

**Bloom's Justification:**
Tests application of the first-principles framework by requiring the learner to reconstruct the procedure sequence — not just recognize it.
