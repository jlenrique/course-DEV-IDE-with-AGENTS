\# \*\*Course 1, Module 1: Foundations of the Innovation Mindset\*\*



\## \*\*Part 1: The Call - Setting the Stage \& Chapter 1\*\*



By the end of this module, the learner will be able to:



1\. \*\*Define\*\* the "innovation mindset" and \*\*correlate\*\* core clinical competencies (e.g. diagnostic reasoning, empathy) directly to established innovation frameworks like Design Thinking. \*(Understand / Apply)\*

2\. \*\*Analyze\*\* the macro-economic and structural trends—including administrative burnout, healthcare consumerism, and technological acceleration—that necessitate intrapreneurial physician leadership. \*(Analyze)\*

3\. \*\*Differentiate\*\* between a superficial "idea" and a rigorously vetted "opportunity" using the core tenets of Sandra Lam's \*Intrapreneurship Formula\*. \*(Analyze / Evaluate)\*

4\. \*\*Evaluate\*\* systemic operational failures within their own clinical environment by conducting root-cause analyses and defending their findings in a peer-reviewed forum (the "M\&M of Innovation"). \*(Evaluate)\*

5\. \*\*Create\*\* a personal innovation baseline (SWOT analysis) and an "Opportunity Radar" that captures and categorizes real-world clinical frictions for future project development. \*(Create)\*

\### Chapter 1 Knowledge Check: Setting the Stage (5 Questions)



\*\*Q1: The Innovation Mindset (Remembering)\*\*



\- \*\*Prompt:\*\* How does this module define the "innovation mindset" for a healthcare professional?

\- \*\*Correct Answer:\*\* The internal conviction that you are a designer of the healthcare system, empowered to bridge the gap between current limitations and future possibilities, rather than just an employee within it.



\*\*Q2: Physician Innovators (Understanding)\*\*



\- \*\*Prompt:\*\* Dr. Peter Pronovost’s implementation of the central line checklist is primarily an example of which type of innovation?

\- \*\*Correct Answer:\*\* Process Innovation.



\*\*Q3: Mental Frameworks (Applying)\*\*



\- \*\*Prompt:\*\* A clinical team experiences an unexpected spike in patient readmissions after implementing a new discharge protocol. Instead of assigning blame, the attending physician says, "This is valuable data showing us where our protocol is fragile." This response best demonstrates which mental framework?

\- \*\*Correct Answer:\*\* Embracing Intelligent Failure.



\*\*Q4: Psychological Safety (Evaluating)\*\*



\- \*\*Prompt:\*\* According to Amy Edmondson, which of the following is the best indicator that a clinical department has high psychological safety?

\- \*\*Correct Answer:\*\* Junior residents frequently point out potential workflow errors to attending physicians without fear of interpersonal penalty.



\*\*Q5: The Hero's Journey (Understanding)\*\*



\- \*\*Prompt:\*\* In the context of the Innovator's "Hero's Journey" outlined in this certificate, what is the primary goal of Course 1?

\- \*\*Correct Answer:\*\* Sparking innovation by learning to see the daily clinical world differently and moving from insight to a rigorously vetted opportunity.
